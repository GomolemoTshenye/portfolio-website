I'm Gomolemo Tshenye — a third-year Computer and Information Science student specializing in Application Development at Varsity College (IIE Emeris) Pretoria. I'm a full-stack developer who thrives on building robust, scalable applications that actually run and solve real-world problems.

Before this degree, I completed a Higher Certificate in Web and Mobile Application Development, which laid a strong foundation in both front-end and back-end architectures. Today, the CS programme provides the depth: I am mastering full-stack development, cloud integration, and UX design through advanced modules (PROG7311, PROG7313, INSY7311). Driven by a commitment to technical excellence, I consistently aim for top-tier performance across all my software projects and portfolios of evidence.

Most of my time outside class goes into bringing complex projects to life. Wallet Willow is a multi-platform personal-budgeting Android app built in Kotlin, specifically targeting students. I led the backend integration alongside my team, utilizing Firebase Auth, Firestore sync, Room for offline storage, and custom Canvas-based chart views. I've also developed SocialSpark, an Android schedule manager focusing on specialized time and date features for student tasks, and EventEase, a venue management system leveraging live Azure Blob Storage and SQL database integration. Most recently, I engineered Overlooked Group, a public-facing corporate .NET web prototype complete with an AI chatbot and a CSR portal.

On the side, I built Lotto Lab — a self-contained statistical analysis and prediction site for the South African National Lottery. It features five predictive models (Frequency, Recency, Poisson, Markov, Ensemble), a walk-forward backtest engine across Lotto, PowerBall, and Daily Lotto, and updated game rules. It started as a curiosity about whether theoretical modelling would survive contact with real draw data. Mostly, it doesn't—which is exactly what makes the data engineering so fascinating.

Alongside my technical work—which is further reinforced by Cisco Networking Academy certifications in Cybersecurity and Introduction to Modern AI—I've spent time in administrative and customer-facing roles. Supporting student enrollment and campus events as a registration assistant has been genuinely useful for the collaboration and communication side of building software. I am actively seeking Work Integrated Learning placements as a Web Developer, and I am highly motivated to pursue immersive graduate leadership programmes to apply these skills at an enterprise level.

What I care about: writing clean, maintainable code that is honest about its limitations; employing strict version control like an adult; and learning a new framework only when the old one stops solving the problem. Long-term, I am aiming at a full-stack engineering or cloud-architecture path.
